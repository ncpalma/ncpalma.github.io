Welcome to my website! I am a Professor at the Department of Politics and Government at Universidad Alberto Hurtado (Santiago, Chile). My research focuses on political and electoral behavior in Latin America. I am particularly interested in political participation, democratic values, and my most recent projects have centered on support for and opposition to the far right in the region.

I hold a PhD (class of 2024) and a Master's degree in Political Science from the Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile, with mentions in Comparative Politics and Research Methods. During my doctoral studies I was an ANID fellow, and I subsequently held a postdoctoral fellowship at the Center for Social Conflict and Cohesion Studies (COES). I received my undergraduate degree in Political Science from Universidad Diego Portales.

I am currently developing a research project funded by Universidad Alberto Hurtado titled: "Dealignment or Realignment? Studying Electoral Behavior in Chile in Comparative Perspective." This project seeks to determine whether the new parties that have emerged in Chile (including the far-right party currently in government) reflect a dealignment or realignment of the electorate. The project draws on the European literature on the far right as well as the emerging regional scholarship on the topic. One of the key contributions of this longitudinal study is understanding where the votes for far-right parties come from in a context where these parties are relatively new in Latin America.

Finally, my teaching centers on quantitative methods. Throughout the year I teach Introduction to Statistics, Basic Quantitative Methods, Advanced Quantitative Methods, and Electoral Analysis and Political Behavior.
